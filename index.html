Areas of Practice

Six disciplines. One standard of preparation.

The chambers accepts briefs across the full spectrum of civil, commercial, criminal, family, real estate and labour matters — before trial courts, tribunals, authorities and in appellate jurisdiction.

I.

Civil & Commercial Law

The core of the chambers' practice — civil rights, property, contract and commercial disputes, prosecuted and defended with disciplined pleadings.

  • Civil suits — injunctions, declarations & recovery
  • Property disputes, land & revenue matters
  • Contract law & commercial litigation
  • Arbitration & alternate dispute resolution
  • Consumer protection & banking law
II.

Criminal Law

Defence at trial and in appellate jurisdiction, grounded in the first principles of criminal procedure and evidence.

  • Bail — regular & anticipatory
  • Trial defence, appeals & revisions
  • FIR quashing before the High Court
  • Economic offences, cyber crime & NDPS matters
  • Matrimonial offences & S. 498A defence
III.

Family & Matrimonial Law

Matrimonial, custody and succession matters handled with discretion, and with a preference for resolution where resolution serves the client.

  • Divorce — mutual & contested; maintenance
  • Child custody & adoption
  • Domestic Violence Act proceedings
  • Court marriages & Special Marriage Act
  • Probate, succession certificates & family settlements
IV.

Real Estate & Infrastructure

The full lifecycle of immovable property — from due diligence before purchase to regulatory relief after possession.

  • RERA proceedings & homebuyer claims
  • Title verification & land due diligence
  • Land acquisition & development permissions
  • Lease, rent & tenancy law
V.

Corporate Advisory & Compliance

Advisory and disputes work for businesses — structured so that compliance is met before it becomes litigation.

Labour Law & Service Matters

Employer- and employee-side representation across the industrial and service law spectrum.

The Name

Ārjava is not a slogan. It is a working method.

"Straightness of mind, speech and act — this is called ārjava." — Classical definition, Sanskrit ethical literature

01 Candour before comfort

A client is told the true strength of the case at the first conference — including what it will cost, how long it will take, and where it can fail.

02 The document is the argument

Cases in the district judiciary are won on the pleadings. Every plaint, written statement and application leaving these chambers is drafted to be decided on a single reading.

03 Preparation as discipline

No appearance without the file mastered. No hearing treated as routine. The chambers treats each date as the date the matter may be decided.

04 The client owns the decision

Counsel advises; the client decides. Every strategic fork in a matter is put to the client in plain language, with the risks of each path stated.

Agra's manufacturing economy — footwear, leather goods, handicrafts — runs substantially on credit terms, and substantially on delayed payments. The Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development Act, 2006 gives registered suppliers a recovery route that ordinary civil suits cannot match.

#### The statutory advantages

Section 16 imposes compound interest, at three times the bank rate notified by the Reserve Bank, from the appointed day. Section 18 permits a reference to the Micro and Small Enterprises Facilitation Council, which proceeds through conciliation and, failing settlement, arbitration. Section 19 requires a buyer challenging the award to deposit seventy-five per cent of the awarded amount before the challenge is entertained — a condition that transforms settlement dynamics.

#### The conditions precedent

The supplier must hold a valid Udyam registration, and must have held it at the time of supply. The claim must arise from goods supplied or services rendered — the regime does not extend to every commercial grievance between the parties.

For an enterprise carrying receivables beyond the statutory period, the reference is frequently the most efficient remedy available — and the least used.
